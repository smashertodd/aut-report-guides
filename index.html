Reports:

Business

Reports communicate findings from research, experiments, or other activities. They are clearly structured, so they can be read quickly.

How to write a report

Step 1: Identify the issue

Reports usually focus on specific issues or situations. For example, a student has to investigate possible solutions for a company's human resource information system:

Figure 1: Example report assessment task

Step 2: Analyse the issue and generate findings and conclusions

You usually need to use concepts and models from the course to analyse an issue, so that you can:

  • generate findings,
  • discuss those findings by connecting them with course readings and other literature,
  • reach conclusions based on the findings, and
  • make recommendations based on the conclusions.
  • Generating findings
  • Planning the discussion
  • Reaching conclusions
  • Making recommendations

Generate your findings by analysing information about an issue. Record your findings in a table or spreadsheet and use them to generate conclusions and recommendations to include in your report:

Figure 2: Connecting a finding with a conclusion and a recommendation

There are multiple methods for analysing information, so check if there is a specific one you need to use.

Create an ideas map to help you organise the discussion because this is where the most detailed information goes. For example, this discussion has four main sub-sections, and some of those have their own sub-sections:

Figure 3: How to organise a discussion section

The conclusions need to be based on your findings. Do not introduce any new points. For each finding, identify its significance. Doing this will help you generate specific conclusions:

Figure 4: Conclusions are based on findings

Recommendations identify specific future actions, and they need to be based on your conclusions. A table can help with matching recommendations to conclusions:

Figure 5: Recommendations are based on conclusions

Step 3: Write your report

Reports usually have distinct sections, with headings and sub-headings. Check your assessment task instructions for the specific sections you need to include.

Examples of common report sections

  • Executive Summary
  • Introduction
  • Discussion
  • Conclusions
  • Recommendations
